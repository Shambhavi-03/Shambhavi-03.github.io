The Sherlock Holmes stories penned by Sir Arthur Conan Doyle introduced readers to one of literature's most iconic rivalries, that between the brilliant detective Sherlock Holmes and the criminal mastermind Professor Moriarty. However, it is important to note that this rivalry was not just one of wits but also rooted in a deeper connection. Here I have explored the nuanced friendship between Sherlock Holmes and Professor Moriarty, drawing insights from both Doyle's original stories and the reinterpretation in the manga and anime series "Moriarty the Patriot."

Sir Arthur Conan Doyle (1859-1930) was a Scottish physician and writer best known for his creation of the iconic fictional detective, Sherlock Holmes. Born in Edinburgh, Doyle's early medical career and experiences as a doctor greatly influenced his writing. His first Holmes story, "A Study in Scarlet," was published in 1887, and Holmes quickly became a literary sensation. Conan Doyle went on to write three more novels and fifty-six short stories featuring the brilliant detective. Beyond Holmes, Doyle was a prolific writer, authoring historical novels, science fiction, and works on spiritualism. Despite his diverse literary contributions, he remains forever associated with the brilliant mind of Sherlock Holmes, a character that continues to captivate readers and inspire adaptations to this day.
